About

We are the Typography Bandit, design vigilantes protecting the internet from bad type choices. We are legion. Before you commit another type crime, please review the following free typo­graphy best practices and resources so together we can make the web a safer place for type to live.

Best Practices

  • Make your body font size at least 17px for desktop screens and never under 16px for mobile screens. Setting your html to 16px and then assigning rem values to your headings and body text is one way to do this. (The body text on this site is set at 1.25rem, or 20px, for larger screens.)
  • For responsive font sizing, consider using the CSS clamp() function. For example, on this site we use clamp(1.1rem,3vw,1.25rem)for our body font. The first value is the minimum font size (for mobile screens), the middle value is the target font size, and the final value is the maximum font size.
  • Leave body text letter spacing alone, i.e. letter-spacing: 0px. Subheadings in all caps may require a little extra spacing.
  • Set your maximum line width to no more than 75 characters. That includes spaces. You can assign rem values to your text container to do this. The text on this site is set at max-width: 40rem, for example. Based on a root font size of 16px, we get a line width of around 640px. That works out to roughly 70 characters per line for desktops with our body text at 20px.
  • Choose background, foreground (text), and accent colors with high contrast for maximum accessibility. Use the WebAIM Contrast Checker (link below) to see if you're in the ballpark.
  • Never justify text, and try to avoid centering body text.
  • Use no more than three, and preferrably two, typefaces on one website. It's not just a question of aesthetics. Loading all those font files will slow down your website and generate more CO2.
  • Use monospace fonts sparingly. They're harder on the eyes.
  • Consider system fonts. Some of them are pretty good. See our link to Modern Font Stacks below.
  • Host your own fonts rather than linking to a CDN. They generally load faster this way and so help with FOIT (Flash of Invsible Text) and FOUT (Flash of Unstyled Text). Just remember to preload your fonts in the head.
